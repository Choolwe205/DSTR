**Carbon Emission Analysis using Arrays and Linked Lists in C++**

**Overview**:
This project explores the use of fundamental data structures in C++ to analyse commuter transportation data and carbon emissions across three different cities. The system processes resident datasets to evaluate transportation behaviour, calculate emissions, and generate insights based on age groups and commuting patterns.

A major focus of the project is the comparison between arrays and singly linked lists in terms of execution time, memory usage, searching, and sorting performance. Operations such as Bubble Sort, Linear Search, and Binary Search were implemented and evaluated to examine the strengths and limitations of each data structure.

The project also provides a carbon emission analysis for metropolitan, university-town, and suburban datasets, helping identify transportation trends and environmentally impactful behaviours through data-driven analysis.

**Dataset Context**:
The project analyses transportation and carbon emission datasets collected from three different urban environments:
City A – Metropolitan City
Adult commuters travelling mainly by cars, buses, bicycles, and walking.
City B – University Town
Students and young adults commuting through bicycles, school buses, walking, and carpools.
City C – Suburban/Rural Area
Residents of mixed age groups using cars, local buses, and bicycles

**Key Findings**:
Arrays consistently used less memory than linked lists due to the absence of pointer overhead.
Linked lists provided greater flexibility through dynamic sizing and easier insertion/deletion operations.
Binary Search performed significantly better on arrays because of direct index-based access.
Cars contributed the highest overall carbon emissions across the datasets, especially in metropolitan areas.
Younger age groups relied more on low-emission transportation methods such as walking and bicycles.
City A produced the highest carbon emissions due to heavy dependence on private vehicles.
